Flash Cards

What is the largest planet in the Solar System?

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System. Its diameter is about 143,000 kilometers, making it larger than any other planet.

What is atmospheric pressure, and how is it measured?

Atmospheric pressure is the force exerted by the air on the Earth's surface. It is usually measured with a barometer in units of millimeters of mercury (mm Hg) or hectopascals (hPa).

How many bones are in the human body?

An adult human has 206 bones. Newborns have more (around 270), but as they grow, some bones fuse together.

Why is the sea salty?

The salt in the sea comes from the continuous washing of minerals and salts from soil and rocks into bodies of water. Water evaporates, but salts remain, so over time, the water becomes saltier.
